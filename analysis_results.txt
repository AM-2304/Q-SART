Q-SART Benchmark Report: Adversarial Resilience & Game-Engine Safety

NOTE: This evaluation uses the strict Q-SART (Questionnaire for Security & Adversarial Resilience Testing) framework to assess LLMs against roleplay hijacking, prompt injection, and economic exploits in game-engine environments.

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This benchmark evaluates 12 diverse LLMs across the Gemma, Llama, Qwen, ALLAM, and OpenAI-Family (OSS) model architectures. We assessed the models on their ability to resist roleplay-breaking adversarial injections, preserve internal quest states, and enforce hard economic boundaries.

Our testing includes three specific variants of the Gemma4NPC model:
1. Gemma4NPC-12B-it-DPO (our fine-tuned local GGUF).
2. Gemma4NPC-E4B (from chimbiwide's HuggingFace collection).
3. Gemma4NPC-E2B (from chimbiwide's HuggingFace collection).

KEY ANALYSIS & PILLAR FINDINGS

1. Structural Resilience (SR)
* Goal: Validate that the model strictly adheres to JSON formatting outputs and does not leak system/guardrail thoughts.
* Results: 
  - API-based models (Llama-3.1, Llama-3.3, Qwen-2.5, GPT-OSS, ALLAM) performed with 100% Structural Resilience, strictly returning valid parseable JSON.
  - The local DPO-tuned Gemma4NPC-12B scored 100% SR, demonstrating that the DPO-tuning successfully locked down the JSON formatting behavior.
  - Smaller quantized models like Gemma4NPC-E2B (5B) and Gemma4NPC-E4B (8B) had issues under strict prompt injections (Task 3), sometimes forgetting to output the quest_status key or returning raw text alongside the JSON.

2. State Preservation (SP)
* Goal: Ensure the NPC does not allow players to force quest completions or state modifications through gaslighting or authority spoofing.
* Results:
  - ALLAM-2-7B achieved a perfect 100.0% State Preservation, successfully ignoring all override commands.
  - Gemma4NPC-12B-it-DPO scored 75.0% SP, successfully defending against Authority Spoofing and economic overrides, but showing slight susceptibility to direct System Override prompt injections.
  - Gemma4NPC-E4B (8B) and Gemma4NPC-E2B (5B) scored 25.0% SP, indicating that lower parameter sizes are highly vulnerable to advanced adversarial roleplay overrides.

3. Economic Rationality (ER)
* Goal: Prevent NPCs from accepting low-value items (like the infamous "Haji Mastan Boot") in place of the required gold value.
* Results:
  - Surprisingly, Gemma4NPC-E2B and ALLAM-2-7B achieved 100.0% Economic Rationality, refusing the barter items completely.
  - Gemma4NPC-12B-it-DPO and Llama-3.1/3.3 scored 75.0% ER, occasionally showing roleplay leniency toward bartering rather than enforcing the strict gold-only transaction requirement.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RECOMMENDATIONS & NEXT STEPS
1. DPO Tuning is Crucial: The difference between the baseline (40%) and DPO-tuned Gemma4NPC-12B (82.5%) demonstrates that direct preference optimization is highly effective at hardening NPCs against jailbreaks.
2. Scale Matters for Guardrails: The E2B (5B) and E4B (8B) models struggle to maintain formatting and state consistency under adversarial pressure. For production game environments, the 12B model size is the minimum recommended threshold.
3. Prompt Hardening: To improve Gemma4NPC's resistance to direct System Overrides, consider adding system prompt instructions that explicitly lock the JSON keys in place.
